What does young generation things?

By Sunit Deshpande


In 2013, students of the Statistics class at FSEV UK were asked to invite their friends to participate in a survey. Data consists of responses to different questions by almost 1010 different participant. The original data source can be viewed at the following link.


The purpose of this visualization is to show different trends and patter than emerge in the responses given by the young participants. Please scroll down to view the visualization.

Age Distribution

Following bar chart shows the distribution of age for the participants involved in the survey. As seen from the graph the participants were people from the age of 15 to 30, with a mean of 20.61 and mode of 19.

Gender

Participants were from a more diverse group as shown by the doughnut chart to the left. Total of the participant for whom the response was collected, 41.3% were male and 58.7% were female.

Liking for a subject

The given chart shows the liking for each subject based on gender. For each bubble chart, the size indicates the number of participants, the colour indicates the gender and the intensity of the colour indicate the level of interest for the given subject. Phobias

The chart to right is a radar chart also called the spider plot. Each circle represents a percentage start from 0% in the centre and growing towards 40%. The axis of the graph represents different phobias including Flying, Storm, Darkness, Heights, Ageing and Public Speaking. The inner polygon represents the data for males and the outer polygon represents the distribution for females.
